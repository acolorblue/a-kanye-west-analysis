His admiration of Malcolm does not stem from an ideological/theoretical analysis of his speeches and seeing how they apply to the current social/economic/political life of black people. -break- Kanye’s love of Malcolm x is the same love he has for donald trump, -break- he simply loves the fact that they both said what they wanted. -break- His last prior reference to trump was in a sermon at his last concert in November 2016 resulting from his anger at radio stations because he felt they just kept playing the same annoying pop music everyone was tired of and he wanted hear more frank ocean and cudi. -break- The excerpt goes “radio fuck you!!! oh yeah i’m on my trump shit tonight! radio fuck you!!!!! yeah i’m taking his lead! imma just say how I say — imma just say — imma just be ye and win! imma take his lead! radio fuck you!!!” yeah that’s that dragon energy shit he talking about. -break- I don’t believe he understands trumps policies whatsoever and I don’t think he truly cares to.

This is also a similar characteristic Malcolm stated he respected in conservatives versus liberals, -break- which is that the conservatives showed their teeth (he expands on this in his fox vs wolf analogy). -break- He stated in a 1964 interview speaking on barry goldwater (a candidate who was backed by the KKK) “if goldwater ever becomes president, -break- one thing his presence in the white house will do — it will make black people in America have to face up to facts probably for the first time in many many years and this in itself is good. -break- In that, -break- goldwater is a man who is not capable of hiding his racist tendencies and at the same time he is not capable of even pretending to negros that he is their friend, -break- so this will have the tendency to make the negro probably for the first time do something to stand on their own feet and solve their own problem instead of putting themselves in a position to be mislead, -break- misused, -break- exploited by the whites who pose as liberals only for the purpose of getting the support of the negro.”

It’s similar to the argument of the honest thief, -break- where one can say “at least he is honest.” (which he isn’t) the difference between Kanye’s and Malcolm’s statement is Kanye doesn’t have the after thought of “this makes it crystal clear as to who our enemies are.” What he fails to realize is, -break- it goes beyond the characteristic. -break- It goes to the children in syria with their hospitals and schools blown up across the street from them. -break- It goes to all the fathers and mothers deported and those constantly living in fear of the abrupt separation of their families forever. -break- That’s family man, -break- that is often times the only thing that gives people some sort of true meaning and worth in their lives. -break- For black people in America police brutality is one of our biggest nuisance and it goes with the enabling of the police officers that support this man and feel empowered to physically exercise their racist views with no repercussions that leaves niggas like me dead with 15 accidental bullets in my chest. -break- He enables that shit and is letting himself be used as a prop while trump throws out the condescending “lowest unemployment” like we all just a whole buncha boot-licking-lazy-watermelon-eating negros waiting for massa to give us something to do.

His motive of love and wanting to unify all people is pure, -break- but he lacks the understanding in realizing the actions and policies of the other side he desires to unify is in complete opposition to the literal survival and liberty of his own people, -break- which was often a critique martin luther king received from Malcolm x. -break- Black people have never had a problem loving. -break- In fact, -break- this contributes to how we ended up in this position, -break- by loving so damn much. -break- These are the same people who get their skulls crushed and turn around and say “why can’t we all just get along?” is it not the natives love that got their land stolen from them? one side made it a war, -break- and they make this very clear in their rhetoric of “taking our country back” — taking back their country from who?? the other side is then by default placed in the position of the opposition in a war which is really just out of self defense and a refusal to be treated as less than human beings or as if their life has no value, -break- this is something so many people who bring the argument of “encouraging dialogue” fail to understand. -break- One side doesn’t add the survival of your life into their agenda and the other is in response simply defending their birth right of liberty & autonomy like what kind of fucking dialogue are you looking for? your freethinking, -break- freedom of speech & "political incorrectness" only becomes a problem when it threatens the actual psychological and physical well-being of other human beings.

(no ableism just analogy) his lack of care for proper information is lazy and dissapointing, -break- yet also plays a bit into our culture of desensitization which we all experience on various levels. -break- In truth we are all victims of witnessing the same devastating scenarios play out over and over again. -break- Do I know how many mass shootings have taken place since the aurora century 16 movie theater shooting?
